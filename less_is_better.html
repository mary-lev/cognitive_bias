Less-is-better effect

Examples of usage

The Less-is-better effect describes a cognitive bias where people judge a smaller quantity as more valuable than a larger one, under certain conditions, contrary to logical expectations. Here are a few examples in one sentence each:

  • Gift Cards: A person may perceive a $50 gift card to a high-end store as more valuable than a $60 gift card to a less prestigious retailer, valuing the exclusivity over the higher monetary value.
  • Food Portions: Individuals might rate a gourmet small portion dish higher in quality and taste than a larger portion of a standard meal, associating size with exclusivity and craftsmanship.
  • Workshops: A shorter, more focused workshop is often seen as more valuable and efficient than a longer, comprehensive course, despite covering less material, due to perceived focus and specificity.
  • Vacations: A shorter vacation to a luxury, exotic destination can be seen as more desirable than a longer stay at a standard vacation spot, with the uniqueness and luxury outweighing the duration.
  • Electronics: A smaller, sleeker electronic gadget is sometimes considered more advanced and desirable than a larger one with more features, associating compactness with modernity and innovation.

Creating an Ontology

Classes:
  • Person (Judge). The individual evaluating and making decisions.
    • hasName
  • Thing. An item or scenario being evaluated.
    • hasDescription
  • Attribute. Characteristics or features of a Thing that are assessed by the Person.
  • Value. The perceived worth or desirability of an Attribute of a Thing.
  • Judgment. The outcome of the evaluation process.
  • RationalDecision (Subclass of Judgment): A decision based on objective evaluation.
  • BiasedDecision (Subclass of Judgment): A decision influenced by the perception that less quantity or size can be more valuable under certain contexts.
Properties (Attributes and Relationships):
  • hasName (Person)
    • Domain: Person
    • Range: String
  • hasAttribute (Thing to Attribute)
    • Domain: Thing
    • Range: Attribute
  • hasValue (Attribute to Value)
    • Domain: Attribute
    • Range: Value
  • makesJudgment (Person to Judgment)
    • Domain: Person
    • Range: Judgment
  • basedOn (Judgment to Thing)
    • Domain: Judgment
    • Range: Thing
    • resultsIn (Judgment to RationalDecision or BiasedDecision)
      • Domain: Judgment
      • Range: RationalDecision, BiasedDecision
