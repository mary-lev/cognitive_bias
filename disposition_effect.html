Desposition Effect

Examples of usage

The disposition effect is a phenomenon observed in behavioral finance where investors have a tendency to sell assets that have increased in value while keeping assets that have decreased in value. This behavior contradicts the rational decision-making model, as it often leads to the realization of smaller gains and larger losses. Here are few examples:

  • Stock Market Trading: Individual Investors: Tendency to sell stocks that have made gains prematurely and hold onto stocks that have declined in value, hoping for a rebound. Professional Traders: Even experienced traders can exhibit the disposition effect, although they may be more aware of it and take steps to mitigate its impact.
  • Real Estate Investment: Selling Properties: Property investors might sell properties that have appreciated in value quicker while holding onto underperforming properties for too long.
  • Cryptocurrency Trading: Given the high volatility, investors might quickly sell off cryptocurrencies that show a slight profit but hold onto losing investments with the hope of a future price increase.
