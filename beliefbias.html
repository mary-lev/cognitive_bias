Belief Bias

Examples of usage

This bias that affects the way we judge the validity of arguments. It occurs when the judgments are influenced more by the believability of the conclusion rather than the logical structure of the argument itself. Here are few examples:

  • Legal Judgments: In court cases, jurors may be influenced by their pre-existing beliefs about the guilt or innocence of a defendant, affecting their ability to impartially evaluate the evidence and arguments presented.
  • Healthcare Decisions: People might accept or reject medical advice based on pre-existing beliefs about health and medicine, rather than on the evidence or logical reasoning supporting that advice. For example, someone might dismiss the benefits of vaccination due to personal beliefs about natural immunity.
  • Financial Decisions: Investment decisions can be influenced by belief bias when investors favor financial advice or opportunities that align with their existing beliefs about the market or certain companies, ignoring logical analysis or evidence to the contrary.
