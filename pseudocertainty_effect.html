Pseudocertainty Effect

Examples of usage

The individuals tend to want to complete a unit of a given item or task, regardless of its size or the quantity involved. This bias can lead to overconsumption or overuse in some contexts, while in others, it might promote efficiency or completion of tasks. Here are few examples:

  • Investment Decisions: Investors may perceive certain investments as 'sure bets' due to past performance or the illusion of stability, ignoring the inherent risks and uncertainties of the market.
  • Insurance Purchases:Individuals might opt for extensive insurance coverage for events they perceive as highly certain to occur, even if the statistical likelihood is low, or conversely, neglect insurance for risks they underestimate.
  • Healthcare Choices: Patients might choose treatments with certain outcomes over those with statistically better outcomes but perceived uncertainty. For example, opting for surgery with a known success rate over a new treatment with potentially better outcomes but less certainty.
